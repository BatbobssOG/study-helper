# Missing Facts — Threaded and Grooved Pipe

Facts provided to be added to the knowledge base.

---

## Iron & Steel

- **The first known purified iron is Pig Iron**
- The percentage of carbon content to manufacture steel pipe is **0.1% to 0.2%**
- **Extra Strong (XS)** pipe is also known as **Extra Heavy (XH)** — NOT extra standard
- **Double Extra Strong (XXS)** pipe is also known as **Double Extra Heavy (XXH)**

---

## Groove and Shoulder System

- The purpose of the gasket is **to prevent the connection from leaking**
- When a groove and shoulder system is first filled with liquid, **some joints may leak for a few seconds until pressure builds in the line** — the gasket is pressure-responsive and seals tighter as pressure increases
- Information stamped on the side of a grooved fitting: **manufacturer's name, style number, and size of fitting**
- Groove system fittings are available in **galvanized, stainless steel, and copper** — all are acceptable
- Using the **wrong gasket** means the **manufacturer will not warranty the system**
- Blueprint symbols used for groove and shoulder fittings are called **grooved joint pipe symbols**
- After cutting pipe to be grooved, you must **ream the inside of both ends** before grooving
- Thermal expansion in piping can be controlled by: **expansion loops**, **slip-type expansion joints**, or **coupling and pipe expansion joints**

---

## Alloy Pipe

- **An alloy pipe is a ferrous pipe** that contains iron and other metals
- Alloying elements may include **copper** and **chromium**

---

## Thread Anatomy

- The **top** of a thread is called the **Crest**
- The **bottom** of a thread is called the **Root**
- The distance between two crests is called the **Pitch**

---

## Fittings

- **Unions** allow for quick assembly and disassembly of piping
- **Nested bushings** are NOT permitted for use on gas lines
- Tees are sized **run × run × branch** — the largest run is listed first (e.g. 1½" × ¾" × ¾" Tee)
- A length of pipe is considered a **nipple** up to and including **12 inches**

---

## Threading

- The R65 hand die must be removed by rotating **counter-clockwise the full length of the thread** so you **do not over-thread** the pipe
- A **universal die head** is used on a **power threading machine**


- The best safeguard against poor threads, heat, and excessive die wear is **a bath in the proper type of threading oil**
- Thread cutting oil must have **good lubricating**, **flushing**, and **cooling** qualities due to the **friction** created between the cutting edge of the die and the pipe
- **Sulphured oils** have a tendency to stain copper and its alloys
- Cutting fluids should be kept **cool** and at a temperature below **110°F / 43°C** while being used for satisfactory tool life
- **Black sulphured cutting oil** is better for heavy duty applications
- An increased viscosity in cutting oil means it has **reduced cooling ability or penetrating power**
- If cooling is of the utmost importance, use **soluble cutting fluid** — petroleum oil mixed with water
- When threading pipe that may contain oxygen, ensure that **none of the lubricant gets inside the pipe** — oxygen and cutting oils can cause explosions

---

## Steel Pipe Applications

Three main applications steel pipe is used for:

1. **Compressed air lines**
2. **Hot water heating systems** — supply and return piping
3. **Gas lines** — from residential gas meters to furnaces and other gas appliances

---

## Pipe End Finishes

Five pipe end finishes available:

1. **Plain**
2. **Threaded**
3. **Threaded and Coupled**
4. **Bevelled**
5. **Grooved**

---

## Pipe Cleaning & Preparation

- Pipe must be clean and free of rust, grease, paint, and other foreign matter — it is **pickled and given a coating of oil** to prevent **corrosion during transit**

---

## Gas Vapor Pressure & Pipe Grade

- Gas vapor operating at pressures over **125 PSI** should be **Extra Strong (XS) grade** unless joints are welded
